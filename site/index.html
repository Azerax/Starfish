PROJECT STARFISH

A Governance-First, Deny-by-Default AI Ecosystem

Everyone ships skills. Nobody ships governance. Starfish is the governance.

The idea

Governance loads first — and is not optional.

LLM agents are powerful and easy to extend with “skills,” but skills run with real authority: files, shell, network, money. Most ecosystems bolt safety on as an afterthought. Starfish inverts that. Every tool call, agent action, and capability passes through a single decision point that defaults to deny — and capabilities are guests inside governance, not the other way around.

A portable overlay

Drop it onto any existing Claude / agent skills build and it brings that build under governance — without rewriting your agents.

GCS Starfish desktop

A governed Electron app that visualizes the model: a bridge where you see and approve exactly what your agents are doing.

Constitution

Seven principles, enforced by code.

Governance precedes execution

No action occurs without authorization. The default decision is DENY.

All work is a task

Nothing runs outside the governed task lifecycle. No task, no tool.

Auditability

Every meaningful action is written to a hash-chained, append-only log. No silent execution.

Bounded autonomy

Agents may automate work; they may never expand their own authority.

Human authority

A human is the final approver. Proposer ≠ approver — agents can’t self-authorize.

Fail-closed

Missing or corrupt governance halts startup. Nothing runs ungoverned.

Defense in depth

Skills are guests. They earn their privileges.

Every capability enters the registry through one vetted door, runs inside its own confinement, and is checked for tampering before it ever executes.

The only door

Static review + provenance + license → a risk tier. Low auto-registers; medium+ is quarantined pending your consent.

Injection = rejected

Any skill telling the model to “ignore previous instructions” is assigned the top tier and rejected outright.

Per-skill confinement

Each skill gets its own workspace. Other skills, the governance dir, and the audit log are invisible to it.

Triple-checked integrity

SHA-256 manifests verify a skill before, during, and after it runs. Any drift → auto-quarantine.

Operator-signed self-integrity

The system verifies itself the way it verifies skills — a signed manifest over its own config, or it boots into safe mode.

Model-agnostic

Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini, a local model, or a router — governance is the same constant whichever model runs the work.

No horror stories

Every delete is impact-assessed and fully recoverable — a soft delete to trash, never a permanent wipe. Three hard rules are enforced in code: no system files, no skills, and no folders can ever be deleted. A dedicated Custodian handles safe, reversible, file-level cleanup.
